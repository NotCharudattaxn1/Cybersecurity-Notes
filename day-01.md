## CIA Triad

### Confidentiality

Only authorized users should have access to information.

Examples:
- Passwords
- Encryption
- MFA

### Integrity

Data should not be modified by unauthorized users.

Examples:
- Hashing
- Digital Signatures

### Availability

Systems should remain accessible whenever authorized users need them.

Examples:
- Redundant servers
- Backups

---

## Authentication

Verifies the identity of a user.

Examples:
- Password
- OTP
- Fingerprint

---

## Authorization

Determines what an authenticated user is allowed to access.

Example:
- Student can view marks.
- Teacher can modify marks.

---

## Threat

Anything capable of causing harm.

Examples:
- Hacker
- Malware
- Flood

---

## Vulnerability

A weakness in the system.

Examples:
- Weak password
- Outdated software

---

## Exploit

A method used to take advantage of a vulnerability.

Example:
- SQL Injection

---

## Risk

Risk = Likelihood × Impact

---

## What I Learned Today

- Difference between Authentication and Authorization.
- Difference between Threat and Vulnerability.
- CIA Triad is the foundation of cybersecurity.
